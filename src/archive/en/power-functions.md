###### **1.5.1 Power Functions with Integer Exponents**

A **power function** is defined as $ f(x) = x^a $, where $ a $ is a constant called the **exponent**. Power functions appear in many real-world contexts, such as:

- **Area of a circle**: $ \\pi r^2 $
- **Edge length of a cube**: $ V^{1/3} $
- **Gravitational force**: Proportional to $ r^{-2} $

The properties of power functions depend on whether the exponent $ a $ is:
1. **Positive or negative**
2. **Integer or non-integer**

#### **Integer Exponents ($ a \\geq 0 $)**
1. When $ a \\geq 0 $, power functions are **polynomials** or **monomials**:
   - $ x^0 = 1 $
   - $ x^1 = x $
   - $ x^2, x^3, \\dots $ are repeated products.
   
2. **Rules of Calculation**:
   - $ x^a \\cdot x^b = x^{a+b} $
   - $ (x^a)^b = x^{a \\cdot b} $
   - $ (xy)^a = x^a \\cdot y^a $

3. **Graph Symmetry**:
   - Even powers ($ x^2, x^4 $): **Symmetric** about the vertical axis ($ f(-x) = f(x) $).
   - Odd powers ($ x^3, x^5 $): **Point-symmetric** about the origin ($ f(-x) = -f(x) $).

#### **Negative Integer Exponents ($ a < 0 $)**
1. Negative powers are defined as:
   - $ x^{-a} = \\frac{1}{x^a} $
   - Examples: $ x^{-1} = \\frac{1}{x}, x^{-2} = \\frac{1}{x^2} $

2. **Graphs**:
   - Graphs have **vertical asymptotes** at $ x = 0 $ and **horizontal asymptotes** at $ y = 0 $.
   - Even negative powers ($ x^{-2}, x^{-4} $): Symmetric about the vertical axis.
   - Odd negative powers ($ x^{-1}, x^{-3} $): Point-symmetric about the origin.

3. **Extended Rule**:
   - $ \\frac{x^a}{x^b} = x^{a-b} $, valid for $ x \\neq 0 $.

#### **Graphs of Power Functions**
- $ x^0 $: Horizontal line $ y = 1 $
- $ x^1 $: Straight line through the origin
- $ x^2 $: Parabola
- $ x^3 $: S-shaped curve
- $ x^{-1} $: Hyperbola
- $ x^{-2} $: Hyperbolic shape with symmetry about the vertical axis

#### **Non-Integer Exponents**
- Not covered in detail in this video; will be addressed in the next lecture.

###### **1.5.2 Power Functions with Integer Exponents**

How can power functions be extended to non-integer exponents?

1. **Definition of Power Functions for Non-Integer Exponents**:  
   - For **positive x-values**, the power function $x^{1/n}$ (where $n$ is a positive integer) is defined as the $n$-th root of $x$.  
   - For example, $x^{1/4}$ is the positive number whose fourth power equals $x$.  
   - These definitions allow for the extension of power functions to fractional exponents, such as $x^{p/q}$:  
     - $x^{p/q} = (x^p)^{1/q} = (\\sqrt[q]{x})^p$.  
     - For negative exponents like $x^{-p/q}$: $x^{-p/q} = 1 / (\\sqrt[q]{x^p})$.  

2. **Graph Characteristics**:  
   - **Positive Exponents**:  
     - For even $n$: The graph is only defined for non-negative $x$.  
     - For odd $n$: The graph is defined for all real $x$.  
     - Odd-power graphs are point symmetric around the origin, while even-power graphs are symmetric about the $y$-axis.  
   - **Negative Exponents**:  
     - The graphs have horizontal asymptotes at $y = 0$ and vertical asymptotes at $x = 0$.  

3. **General Exponents**:  
   - If $a$ is positive, $x^a$ is defined for $x \\geq 0$.  
   - If $a$ is negative, $x^a$ excludes $x = 0$.  
   - For some non-integer exponents (e.g., $a = p/q$ with odd $q$), $x^a$ can be defined for negative $x$, but such cases are rare.  

4. **Rules of Calculation**:  
   - The previously established rules for integer exponents (e.g., $x^a \\cdot x^b = x^{a+b}$) also apply to positive $x$ for general exponents $a$ and $b$.  
   - However, complications arise for negative or non-integer exponents when $x$ is negative or zero.  

5. **Practical Application**:  
   - The behavior of power functions for general exponents is significant in mathematical analysis.  
   - To fully grasp these properties, completing related exercises is recommended.
